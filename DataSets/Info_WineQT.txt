Target: Quality

Columns:
Fixed acidity: Amount of non-volatile acids in wine, contributing to taste and stability.

Volatile acidity: Amount of acetic acid; high levels can cause an unpleasant vinegar taste.

Citric acid: Contributes to freshness and flavor balance in wine.

Residual sugar: Amount of sugar remaining after fermentation; affects sweetness.

Chlorides: Salt content in wine; impacts taste.

Free sulfur dioxide: SO₂ present that can prevent microbial growth and oxidation.

Total sulfur dioxide: Total SO₂ (bound + free); affects preservation and stability.

Density: Mass per unit volume; influenced by sugar and alcohol content.

pH: Measures acidity/alkalinity; affects taste and stability.

Sulphates: Contribute to wine’s flavor and act as preservatives.

Alcohol: Alcohol content (%) of the wine; affects body and flavor.

Quality: Sensory score of wine quality, ranging from 0 (poor) to 10 (excellent).
